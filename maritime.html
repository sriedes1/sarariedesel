Maritime College

Renewable Energy

Maritime College has been installing a solar thermal domestic hot water unit the past three years. It is officially 99% complete as of early 2016 and is one of the largest on the east coast. The system provides hot water to all the dorms and dining facilities on campus. During the summer months, the solar thermal system allows the campus to shut down their fossil fuel powered water heater. In total, the system saves the college approximately $300,000 each summer. This savings does not include the cost of being able to shut down the other fossil fuel plant and not pay the workers or any of the costs affiliated with running a plant. They are currently looking at building a wind turbine since they are on the water and are located in a good wind region. However, the set-back to this is that turbines are extremely loud and the College is located in a residential area.

Research

The main research at Maritime College is studying the payback time each form of renewable energy has. Their current research has proven the College should not invest in photovoltaics since they payback is not adequate to support installation costs. In general, they found the period would be 17-19 years on a panel that would only last 20 years maximum. The payback period for their solar thermal unit is only 6 years.

Renewable Energy Credits

As of 2015, Maritime College is not purchasing Renewable Energy Credits nor are they producing enough renewable energy to create their own Renewable Energy Credits. If you would like to know more about Maritime or would like to encourage them to buy RECs/ produce more renewable energy, please contact them at admissions@sunymaritime.edu or visit sunymaritime.edu
