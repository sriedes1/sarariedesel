Morrisville State College

Renewable Energy

In 2003, Morrisville installed a 10kW wind turbine located near the dairy complex. The turbine is estimated to save the College $600 annually. The other unique source of renewable energy at Morrisville State is their Anaerobic Methane Digester. The system treats manure from 400 cows and generates 350,000 kWh annually from recovered biogas. The College plans on implementing solar panels and hydropower in the near future.

Research

There is some sustainable research occurring on campus, but they would like to eventually do more. Some of the recent sustainable/environmental research project at Morrisville have been How Green is Central New York, Energy Alternatives, and The Future of Farming and Food. The College also offers multiple courses in sustainability and environmental studies.

Renewable Energy Credits

Morrisville State College is not purchasing Renewable Energy Credits and they are barely producing enough renewable energy to create their own Renewable Energy Credits. If you would like to know more about Morrisville State or would like to encourage them to buy RECs/ produce more renewable energy, please contact them at admissions@morrisville.edu or visit morrisville.edu
