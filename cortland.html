State University College at Cortland

Renewable Energy

A $3 million solar project included 3,604 solar panels that produce 1.5 million kWh of energy. These solar panels are located by the entrance to the University and on the roofs of the Park Center Alumni Area. This solar array powers 6% of the University’s electricity. They are planning to get 10% solar by 2050. The University also has 40 wells for a Geothermal Heat Pump.

Research

There is some research occurring at SUNY Cortland, but there is none that is specifically focused on sustainability efforts or environmental topics. The University recently has added a master’s degree in Sustainable Energy Systems.

Renewable Energy Credits

SUNY Cortland is the first SUNY campus to power all of their buildings by renewable energy. Most of this is done by purchasing Renewable Energy Credits. They do this by paying a premium through their energy provider. The University is producing enough renewable energy to create their own Renewable Energy Credits, but is not currently selling them. If you would like to know more about SUNY Cortland or would like to encourage them to buy RECs/ produce more renewable energy, please contact them at admissions@cortland.edu or visit Cortland.edu
